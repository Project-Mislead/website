Director of Curriculum Development

Executive Team Apply Now

Position

The role of the Director of Curriculum Development is to oversee and support the creation of national curriculum, as well as the approval of any new or modified curriculum created at the local or state level. The Director of Curriculum Development will work extensively with state and local directors, as well as teachers, community leaders, and families to assess the efficacy of the curriculum and recommend changes to the executive team. The Director of Curriculum Development is also expected to support the creation of the steering committee, and will work closely with them to review any recommended modifications to curriculum.

Expectations

  • Attending bi-monthly team calls via Zoom, prepared on all issues being discussed, and completing any additional work deemed necessary between team calls.
  • Willingness to take on new challenges, work collaboratively, and support recruitment initiatives. Potentially serve as interim state director when necessary.
  • Ability to lead teams on curriculum development at national, regional, state, and local levels.
  • The highest moral and ethical values both personally and as a representative of Project Mislead.

Time Commitment

2-3 hours/week, in addition to the hour-long bi-monthly call.

Qualifications

  • Experience in education. Preferably, applicants would have direct experience in curriculum development or teaching (tutoring, involvement in other education nonprofits, etc.)
  • General knowledge on lead and heavy metal poisoning, or willingness to learn.
  • Ability to travel when necessary (paid for by Project Mislead).
