And yet, nothing has been done.

Project Mislead was born out of silence - something billions endure globally, politically, socially, and educationally, on lead and heavy metal poisoning. Despite being easily avoided through home repairs, broad awareness of common contaminants like paint, and knowledge of safe practices, it’s become a “silent epidemic”; one that affects 800 million children in the world, including 1.2 million in the US. Still, only 10 states nationally make lead testing for children mandatory - meaning that for families who don’t know about its risks, toxicity their children grow up around goes unchecked. Thousands of children yearly face cognitive delays, language impediments, developmental harms, and in severe cases, organ failure. But because the issue isn’t flashy, because its effects aren’t obvious, because the poorest and most diverse communities face the brunt of the issue - no one knows.

It’s time for a change.


While we were born out of silence, our values are the opposite. We make change by teaching others. We know that every single community, from Charlotte County to Colorado Springs to the Cuyahoga Valley, is impacted differently. For this reason, we recruit ambassadors and local volunteers in specific regions nationally - schools, small towns, suburbs, or cities - and we train them to make a difference. We give them the materials, funding, and connections they need to understand the issues their region faces and teach others. No other national organization is dedicated to this - taking an issue as diverse and multifaceted as lead and heavy metal poisoning - and solving it, one school, community, county, or city at a time. We channel the existing power of grassroots organizers, community leaders, and passionate youth into our organization, while giving them the support and freedom they need to make a difference.

We can’t expect change to be made in an instant. That’s the problem with existing initiatives. By being the first to unify educational initiatives and empower local leaders - we know we can change the world. After all, we’re leading the way to a better future for all.
